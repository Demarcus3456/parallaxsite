KFC

Section One

KFC is an acronym for Kentucky Fried Chicken, a popular American fast food restaurant chain. It specializes in chicken and is represented by Colonel Harland Sanders, the man who created the chain. The franchise opened its first location in 1952 and quickly grew in popularity as an alternative to the hamburger. KFC helped popularize fried chicken and the chain moved to locations around the world. KFC is one of several acronyms and abbreviations for fast food chains, along with DQ, mcds, and BK.
